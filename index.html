Chamber II — The Procession

Every pilgrimage begins
by leaving the light.

Forty columns carry a ceiling no one has ever seen. They were raised by hands that believed weight could be a prayer. Walk between them slowly. The dark ahead of you is not empty — it is waiting.

Chamber III — The Veiled

The gods did not die.
They were forgotten,
which is slower.

Three figures. Marble. Heights unrecorded. When the last worshipper left, the sanctuary covered its own statues — not to protect the stone, but to spare it the sight of an empty door.

  • iThe Witnesssaw everything, and was asked nothing.
  • iiThe Orantstill mid-prayer; the request survives, the language does not.
  • iiiThe Mournergrief that has outlived its cause, and stayed.

Chamber IV — The Fallen

Even ruin
is a form of architecture.

The roof failed in one night, in the year the record ends. The columns lie exactly where they landed, keeping the grammar of their falling. Nothing here has been restored. Restoration is a kind of forgetting.

Chamber V — The Winged One

One of them
refused to be forgotten.

She has no face, and needs none. Sixteen metres of wing, never veiled — the shroud would not stay on. She is not waiting to be worshipped. She is waiting for you to look up.

Chamber VI — Anabasis

Go up.
Tell them the stone is still here.
